IDANÄS WARDROBE
----------------
- Added IKEA IDANÄS wardrobe article 604.588.35.
- Published outer dimensions are represented exactly: 121 cm wide, 59 cm deep and 211 cm high.
- Added a dedicated closed-door model rather than reusing a generic wardrobe block.
- Model includes:
  - full-size top cap
  - side carcass panels and back panel
  - lower frame and four feet
  - four folding-door leaves
  - recessed front panels and raised rails
  - four small front knobs
- All model parts remain inside the exact 121 × 59 × 211 cm planning volume.
- Door opening is not animated because the current request concerns floor planning and wall placement.
- Added the official IKEA product page to the furniture catalogue, source CSV and public README.

AUTOMATIC WARDROBE WALL SNAP
----------------------------
- Added automatic wall alignment to IDANÄS, PAX and BRIMNES wardrobes.
- Snapping is based on the measured six-segment room polygon rather than an axis-aligned room box.
- Each wall has an explicit inward-facing wardrobe rotation:
  Wall 1: 0°
  Wall 2: -90°
  Wall 3: 0°
  Wall 4: -90°
  Wall 5: 180°
  Wall 6: 90°
- The wardrobe centre is positioned using half its depth plus a 0.8 cm planning gap, so its back sits flush without intersecting the wall.
- Along-wall placement is clamped so the entire wardrobe width remains on the selected wall segment.
- Wall segments too short for a wardrobe are rejected as snap candidates.
- The nearest valid wall candidate is selected within a 38 cm centre-distance threshold.

MANUAL OVERRIDE
---------------
- Q/E, Shift+Q/E and exact numeric rotation switch a wardrobe to manual wall-snap override.
- If the wardrobe was already flush to a wall, manual rotation shifts it inward by the additional rotated wall-normal extent before collision validation.
- This prevents the new orientation being immediately forced back to the wall-aligned angle.
- W or the visible wall-snap button restores automatic alignment.
- The active wall-snap state is displayed in both the selection banner and the Selected object panel.

- Existing non-wardrobe furniture movement is unchanged.
