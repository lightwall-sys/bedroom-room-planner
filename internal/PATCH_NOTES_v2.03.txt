BEDROOM ROOM PLANNER v2.03 — INTERNAL PATCH NOTES
==================================================

SOURCE LINEAGE
--------------
Direct successor to Bedroom Room Planner v2.02.
The v2.01 startup recovery and the v2.02 single-authority render scheduler and directional cutaway logic are retained.

1. REPEATING HORIZONTAL WALL PATTERN
------------------------------------
Observed behaviour:
- Large wall surfaces still showed dense, repeating horizontal and stepped bands after the v2.02 bias adjustment.
- The regular pattern and block boundaries were characteristic of shadow-map self-shadow acne rather than a wall image texture.

Root cause:
- Visible wall meshes were still both receiving and writing the overhead spotlight shadow map.
- Large double-sided wall boxes could therefore self-shadow at shallow angles even with positive bias and normalBias.

Fix:
- Visible wall meshes no longer cast shadows.
- Walls still receive legitimate shadows from furniture, trim and fixed objects.
- Non-wall room geometry continues to cast shadows normally.
- Wall materials remain completely texture-free, fully rough and dithered.

Relevant construction:
- model batch mesh.castShadow assignment
- baseMaterial() wall branch

2. WHITE-WALL AND LIGHTING BALANCE
----------------------------------
- Changed the non-accent wall base from warm grey-white to a neutral near-white.
- Preserved exactly one selected accent wall; setAccentWall() continues to repaint every other wall with C.wall_white.
- Added a HemisphereLight for soft sky/ground fill.
- Increased neutral ambient fill and made it time-of-day aware.
- Reduced the muddy/dim character of white walls without flattening object shadows.
- Raised and stabilised exposure slightly across the day cycle.

Relevant settings:
- C.wall_white
- wall emissive intensity
- ambient and hemisphere lights
- applyTimeOfDay()
- renderer.toneMappingExposure

3. ROTATION MODEL
-----------------
New default behaviour:
- Q / E and the main on-screen controls rotate floor furniture or the bed by 90 degrees.
- Shift+Q / Shift+E and the fine on-screen controls rotate by 5 degrees.
- R snaps the active floor object, placement ghost or bed to the nearest 90 degrees.
- Exact angle fields remain available for direct numeric entry.

Movement refinement:
- Arrow keys continue to nudge by the active grid size.
- Shift+arrow keys always nudge by 1 cm, allowing a large wardrobe to be moved gradually until a fine-angle rotation becomes valid.

Relevant constants/functions:
- ROTATE_SNAP_DEGREES
- ROTATE_FINE_DEGREES
- rotationStep()
- nearestRightAngle()
- rotateBed()
- rotatePending()
- rotateSelectedItem()
- snapActiveRotation()
- document keydown handler

5. DELETION AND STALE SHADOWS
-----------------------------
Root cause:
- removeSelectedItem() removed the scene object but did not mark the manually managed shadow map dirty.
- With renderer.shadowMap.autoUpdate disabled, the removed object's previous shadow could remain visible until another shadow-refreshing action occurred.

Fix:
- Clear the selection helper before removal.
- Remove item meshes from the raycast registry.
- Dispose the removed proxy geometry/materials.
- Mark the shadow map dirty, request a multi-frame shadow render and commit history immediately.
- Added visible Delete controls and Delete/Backspace documentation.

Relevant function:
- removeSelectedItem()
