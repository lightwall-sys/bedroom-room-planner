IDANÄS MODEL MAINTENANCE
------------------------
The item definition key is `idanaes` in `itemDefs`.

Published dimensions:
- width: 121 cm
- depth: 59 cm
- height: 211 cm
- article: 604.588.35

The `buildProxy()` IDANÄS branch deliberately keeps every component inside the published bounding volume. The full-depth top cap establishes the exact ±60.5 cm width, ±29.5 cm depth and 211 cm height. Do not add handles, moulding or door details beyond those bounds without also changing the collision footprint and product claim.

The four front leaves are a visual planning approximation of the closed folding doors. Door-opening clearance and hinge animation are not currently modelled.

WARDROBE FRONT DIRECTION
------------------------
For all floor proxies, local +Z is the front and local -Z is the back. Automatic wall snapping depends on this convention.

Wall-aligned rotations:
- Wall 1: 0°
- Wall 2: -90°
- Wall 3: 0°
- Wall 4: -90°
- Wall 5: 180°
- Wall 6: 90°

Do not reverse the IDANÄS door geometry to local -Z unless every wall-snap rotation is also revised.

WALL-SNAP FUNCTIONS
-------------------
- `supportsAutoWallSnap(item)`
- `floorWallSnapCandidates(item, rawX, rawZ)`
- `nearestFloorWallSnap(item, rawX, rawZ)`
- `applyFloorItemPosition(item, rawX, rawZ, allowWallSnap)`
- `wallNormalExtent(item, rotation, wall)`
- `detachFromSnappedWall(item, wall, oldRotation, newRotation)`
- `wallSnapLabel(item)`
- `toggleActiveWallSnap()`

Constants:
- `AUTO_WALL_SNAP_GAP = 0.8`
- `AUTO_WALL_SNAP_DISTANCE = 38`

The candidate system uses the actual room segments:
- Wall 1: x 0..W1 at z 0
- Wall 2: z 0..W2 at x W1
- Wall 3: x W1..W5 at z W2
- Wall 4: z W2..W6 at x W5
- Wall 5: x 0..W5 at z W6
- Wall 6: z 0..W6 at x 0

A candidate is discarded when the wall segment cannot hold the object's full width plus a 2 cm end margin or when the resulting polygon is outside the measured room.

MANUAL ROTATION CONTRACT
------------------------
Manual rotation must win over automatic wall alignment.

For compatible wardrobes:
- `wallSnapOverride === false` means dragging near a wall may set a canonical wall rotation.
- `wallSnapOverride === true` means pointer movement preserves the user's angle and uses ordinary grid positioning.
- Q/E, Shift+Q/E and numeric angle entry set the override.
- W or the visible wall-snap control clears the override.

When a snapped wardrobe rotates, `detachFromSnappedWall()` moves its centre inward by the difference between its old and new wall-normal extents. Do not remove this without replacing it with another method that prevents the object rotating through the wall.

STATE FIELDS
------------
Floor wardrobes may carry:
- `autoWallSnap`
- `wallSnapOverride`
- `snappedWall`

`wallSnapOverride` and `snappedWall` are serialised in planner history. Old v2.03 history entries remain compatible because missing values default to false/null during rebuild.
